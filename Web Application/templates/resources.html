Resources

  • Free Telephone Counseling: This 24/7 hotline provides free and confidential support for people in distress, as well as resources for loved ones and professionals.

  • Immediate Help: This page provides information on how to get help in a crisis, including hotlines, crisis centers, and emergency services.

While seeking professional help is important, there are also steps you can take to support yourself or someone you know who may be struggling with suicidal thoughts:

  • Encourage open and honest communication: It can be difficult to talk about suicidal thoughts, but creating a safe and supportive environment can help someone feel comfortable opening up.
  • Offer practical support: This can include helping with daily tasks, such as grocery shopping or running errands, or assisting with finding and accessing mental health resources.
  • Practice self-care: This can include engaging in activities you enjoy, spending time with loved ones, and prioritizing your mental and physical health.
  • Remove access to means: If someone is actively suicidal, it is important to remove any access to firearms, medications, or other items that could be used for self-harm.
